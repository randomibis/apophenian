3. Please enter a brief artist’s statement or information about your work/studio practice.

The observation and gathering of discarded objects, the detritus of excess, is the foundation of my work. Expressing the wonder and horror of consumption has re-invigorated my practice, the use of free materials both a restriction and freedom.

This has led me to many realizations about the deep contrast evident in the microcosm of where I live in America, and the intense juxtaposition of wealth and poverty.

I want to use a permanent studio space to explore these dynamics and connect with a broader community and audience. My folk/punk style of expressing these concepts invites the viewer to feel empowered to find their own connection to the conversation. The themes I explore are the questions of the mundane self, and the mystical world. Seeing everyday life as a narrative, the people surrounding us as characters, and the creatures and objects that we interact with as having a ’life/spirit’ that can be transformative. An embracing of Klaus Conrad’s ‘unmotivated seeing of connections’, noticing the synchronicity in interactions and discoveries, an affectionate and curious apophenia mindset.

4. Is there any additional information that you feel would be relevant for Cooperative’s review panel?

As I am only recently able to officially work in the USA, due to my visa status, (receiving a green card in late 2024), I am very ready to return to a disciplined practice, having a compulsion and motivation to expand on concepts, and an opportunity to share ideas in a space that I hope is both welcoming and challenging. My practice currently feels reactionary, and while the impulsiveness of creativity is a great thing, I believe a long-term studio residency would afford the removal of the confining parameters of space, and isolation, allowing a fuller and deeper realization of my concepts.

Coming into a new country I have found the thing I am best at contributing within a community is time, openness, and an ability to find conversation and objective alignment with others.

I feel strongly that working in isolation in any discipline, removes the connections to the diversity of cultural conversation, resulting in art that may struggle to find broader audience connection.
